Stir-Fry

Description:

Chicken stir-fry is a quick and flavorful dish made by cooking bite-sized pieces of chicken in a hot pan with colorful vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, and onions. The chicken is sautéed until golden and tender, then tossed with the vegetables in a savory sauce made from soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a hint of sweetness. The stir-fry is cooked at high heat to retain the crunchiness of the vegetables while ensuring the chicken is juicy and full of flavor. This versatile dish can be served over rice or noodles for a satisfying, balanced meal.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

• 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced into bite-sized pieces

• 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)

• 1 bell pepper, sliced

• 1 medium carrot, julienned

• 1 onion, sliced

• 2 cloves garlic, minced

• 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced

• 1/4 cup soy sauce (low-sodium recommended)

• 1 tablespoon honey or sugar

• 1 tablespoon rice vinegar or lime juice

• 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

• Salt and pepper, to taste

• Cooked rice or noodles (for serving)

Instructions:

• Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

• Add sliced chicken and cook until golden brown and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside.

• In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed, and sauté the bell pepper, carrot, and onion for 3-4 minutes until tender but still crisp.

• Add garlic and ginger, cooking for 1 more minute until fragrant.

• In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and cornstarch (if using).

• Return the chicken to the pan and pour the sauce mixture over everything. Stir well to coat and cook for 2-3 more minutes, until the sauce thickens and everything is well coated.

• Season with salt and pepper to taste.

• Serve the stir-fry over rice or noodles and enjoy!
